Ethical Theories Comparison Chart

Utilitarianism

Core Principle: Greatest good for the most people.

Strengths:
  • Outcome-focused
  • Flexible
  • Common in policy & tech ethics
Weaknesses:
  • May justify harm to individuals
  • Hard to predict consequences
  • Ignores intent

Best For: Algorithms, automation, business decisions

Deontology

Core Principle: Follow moral rules regardless of outcomes.

Strengths:
  • Respects rights
  • Consistent
  • Strong on justice
Weaknesses:
  • Rigid
  • May ignore impact

Best For: Privacy, consent, whistleblowing

Virtue Ethics

Core Principle: Focus on moral character.

Strengths:
  • Encourages integrity
  • Long-term development
  • Flexible
Weaknesses:
  • Can be vague
  • Not always actionable

Best For: Leadership, workplace conduct

Social Contract Theory

Core Principle: Uphold shared rules for fairness.

Strengths:
  • Encourages cooperation
  • Respects agreements
  • Supports fairness
Weaknesses:
  • Assumes shared agreement
  • May ignore minority voices

Best For: Digital rights, governance, communities
